Employment Support

Click on each box to access the link.



Disability Rights California

Information about employment services in the state of California.

Neurodiversity Pathways
A program providing unemployed and underemployed autistic and neurodivergent people with a college education with the opportunity to be part of a simulated work experience. This particular program seems to include links to companies, and their "Neurodiversity Integration Readiness Services" include candidate matching to available jobs. Based in the Bay Area and Silicon Valley.


Department of Rehabilitation

Provides many employment-related supports.
"Note that the DOR has also been known to provide tuition support for disabled students. See "Financial Aid/Funding" below.

Community & Employment Services
A job coaching program that helps people with disabilities find work. CES has not historically worked with clients coming from a university background, but I understand they are now starting to take clients with college educations. CES is also called Progressive Employment Concepts. Unlike the Neurodiversity Pathways program, CES is based in Davis.

Stanford Neurodiversity Project

Many resources related to employment. This includes events (the Stanford Neurodiversity Summit in Oct. 2020 was recorded and should be available online for free) and a registry in which neurodivergent job applicants can list themselves.

AAPD Summer Internship Program

Program for college students and graduates with disabilities

Paid Internship Program

Administered through the regional centers (see general section on "adult transition above). Can cover up to $10,400 in wages annually for internships, but only at programs meeting certain criteria, like the Livermore program mentioned immediately above.

Lawrence Livermore National Laboratories

Has a program to hire neurodivergent students for internships. This is through the Regional Center Paid Internship Program
