Intersectionality

Autism intersects with many other identities - indeed, autistic people are actually more likely to be LGBTQIA+ than neurotypical people. Cisgender women are less likely than cisgender men to receive an autism diagnosis - the ratio is about 4:1 - but it is likely that many autistic women go undiagnosed due to biases and stereotypes, such that the true ratio is probably just 3:1. Furthermore, although there are ethnic and racial differences in access to autism diagnoses, there is little reason to believe ethnic groups differ greatly in true rates of autism.
